SWARALIPI

The Musical Notation

A Film About Art, Sacrifice, and the Price of Devotion

Genre: Musical Drama | Runtime: 150-165 minutes | Language: Bengali/Hindi

The Story

A devoted Rabindra sangeet singer rises from the lanes of Kolkata to become the celebrated voice of tradition, only to watch her world crumble when her filmmaker husband's betrayal, her own artistic compromises, and a rapidly changing music industry strip away everything she holds sacred, leading to one final, fatal performance that becomes her redemption.

Three-Act Synopsis

Act One: The Making of a Nightingale (1970-1983)

Young Ananya Sen discovers her calling when she hears Rabindra sangeet on the radio during Durga Puja. Trained by legendary guru Malabika Mukherjee, she becomes a devoted purist. At 23, she falls in love with Vikram Chatterjee, a charismatic film director from Mumbai, and marries him despite her guru's warnings. She moves to Mumbai, carrying her tanpura and her dreams.

Act Two: Rise and Fall (1983-2003)

Ananya becomes "The Nightingale of Tagore," touring nationally while balancing motherhood poorly. At her career peak‚Äîwinning the Cine Playback Award, and sadly she discovers Vikram's affair with actress Rhea Kapoor. The betrayal triggers a 20-year spiral: Vikram continues the affair openly, her voice fails from stress and alcoholism, the music industry shifts to pop, her son resents her, and she loses everything. After a suicide attempt, she returns to Kolkata, broken and voiceless.

Act Three: Redemption (2003-2012)

Desperate, Ananya records fusion albums that betray her artistic soul but revive her career commercially. She reconciles with her estranged son and gets sober. At 52, knowing her heart is failing, she announces one final concert: a return to pure Rabindra sangeet at Rabindra Sadan. Against medical advice, she performs transcendently, reclaiming her true self. She collapses during the final song "Ekla Chalo Re" and dies in her son's arms‚Äîhaving given everything to her music.

Main Characters

ANANYA SEN (Protagonist)

Age Range: 10-52 (1970-2012)

Pure-hearted Rabindra sangeet devotee who sacrifices everything for her art. Passionate, stubborn, emotionally vulnerable. Her voice is both her identity and her curse. A woman torn between tradition and survival, devotion and compromise.

Arc: Innocent artist ‚Üí Celebrated performer ‚Üí Broken woman ‚Üí Commercial sellout ‚Üí Redeemed purist

MALABIKA MUKHERJEE (Mentor)

Age: 70s-80s

Renowned Rabindra sangeet guru and strict traditionalist. Mother figure to Ananya. Dies midway through the story, leaving Ananya spiritually unmoored. Represents the pure artistic tradition.

VIKRAM CHATTERJEE (Antagonist/Husband)

Age: 32-61

Charismatic film director from Mumbai. Artistic but self-destructive, serial philanderer. Represents modernity and the corruptive influence of commercial success. His betrayal sets Ananya's downfall in motion.

ARIJIT SEN (Son)

Age: Born 1988, ages 0-24 in film

Resents mother's absences during childhood. Becomes her savior in her darkest hour. Software engineer representing new India. His reconciliation with Ananya provides emotional catharsis.

RHEA KAPOOR (The Other Woman)

Age: Late 20s-40s

Glamorous Bollywood actress. Everything Ananya is not‚Äîmodern, secular, unbound by tradition. Vikram's affair partner and later companion.

SOUMEN DAS (Music Producer)

Age: 30s

Young music producer who pushes Ananya toward commercial fusion. Represents the commodification of art. Not villainous, but pragmatic about the industry.

Core Themes

The Price of Art

What does artistic devotion cost? Ananya gives her marriage, her motherhood, her health, and ultimately her life to music. The film asks: is such sacrifice noble or tragic? Can we judge those called to art by ordinary standards?

Tradition vs. Modernity

Rabindra sangeet represents unchanging artistic purity. Fusion music represents commercial adaptation. The film mirrors India's own struggle‚Äîhow do ancient traditions survive in a globalized world? Must they change to endure, or does change destroy their essence?

Women and Sacrifice

Society expects women to be everything‚Äîperfect mothers, devoted wives, successful professionals. Ananya fails at being all three and is punished for it. Yet men face no such expectations. The film unflinchingly shows this double standard.

Identity and Compromise

Who are we without our art? When Ananya loses her voice, she loses herself. When she compromises her artistic values for commercial success, she gains fame but loses her soul. Her journey is about reclaiming authentic identity.

Legacy and Immortality

Ananya dies, but her music lives on. The young girl at the film's end suggests that art transcends death. Perhaps the sacrifice was worth it‚Äînot for Ananya's happiness, but for the beauty she left behind.

Production Strategy

  • Cast: Primarily Bengali/Hindi speaking actors. Lead role requires 4 actors (child, young adult, middle-aged, older). Focus on theatrical actors with musical ability.
  • Shooting Format: Digital cinema cameras (BMPCC 6K or similar) to maximize quality while controlling costs
  • Schedule: 30-35 day shoot over 6-7 weeks. Block shooting by location to minimize company moves.
  • Music: Original Rabindra sangeet performances (public domain). One fusion track to be composed. Live recording during performance scenes for authenticity.
  • VFX: Minimal. Period details through practical effects, costume, and color grading.
  • Post-Production: 3-4 months for editing, color grading, sound design, music mixing

Why This Film, Why Now

Cultural Urgency

Traditional art forms like Rabindra sangeet face extinction in the streaming age. This film documents and celebrates this heritage before it's lost. Tagore's 163rd birth anniversary (2024) has renewed interest in his work globally.

Market Opportunity

  • Growing appetite for regional Indian cinema on global platforms (RRR, Kantara)
  • Streaming services actively seeking diverse cultural content
  • Bengali diaspora represents significant untapped audience
  • Female-driven dramas consistently perform at festivals

Social Relevance

  • Women's stories: Global demand for complex female protagonists beyond stereotypes
  • Mental health: Honest portrayal of depression, addiction, recovery resonates today
  • Work-life balance: The impossible demands on women to "have it all"
  • Artistic integrity: In the age of viral content, a meditation on deep craft matters

Artistic Distinction

Unlike typical biopics, this is character-driven fiction that feels lived-in and real. The use of Tagore's actual songs provides authentic cultural texture. The 42-year span allows for genuine character evolution rarely seen in Indian cinema.

Director's Bio

Indira Dhar

Award-Winning Filmmaker

Indira Dhar has swiftly become a compelling voice in Indian cinema, blending emotional depth with global appeal. Her debut Bengali feature Putul (2024), about the lives of street children, premiered to a standing ovation at Cannes Film Festival and was considered for the 97th Academy Awards in three categories. It had a special screening at IFFI Goa, 2025.

In 2025, she ventured into Hindi cinema with Echoes of Valour, a poignant biopic starring Divya Dutta and Neeraj Kabi, which was unveiled at Cannes, had a closed-door screening at Venice, and is now touring the international festival circuit.

With Swaralipi, Dhar returns to her Bengali roots while exploring universal themes of artistic devotion, sacrifice, and the eternal struggle between tradition and modernity.
